Center for Disease Control (CDC) COVID-19 Information

PREVENTION

COVID-19 is known to be transmitted by aerosols and droplets expelled from the respiratory tract of an infected person coughing, sneezing, breathing, or speaking. The most effective means to mitigate the spread of SARS-CoV-2 is a combination of social distancing measures and the use of face masks. As with many common seasonal viruses, proper personal hygiene measures are also critical. Important modes of monitoring the virus include testing and contact tracing those who have been potentially exposed to an infected individual. Without a widespread treatment or a vaccine, these modes of mitigation are especially significant in controlling the spread of SARS-CoV-2.

To mitigate the transmission of the virus individuals should
  • Wear mask covering their mouth AND nose whenever around others
  • Stay home when possible
  • Social distance with a minimum of 6 ft distance between you and others
  • Wash your hands for 20 seconds after coming in contact with outside objects

    • EXPOSURE PROCEDURE

    If you have come in close contact with anyone who has a positive confirmed COVID-19 test or has been experiencing symptoms, its important to distance yourself from loved ones and begin self-isolating. After isolating for about 4-5 days, to account for the incubation period of the virus, schedule an appointment with your primary care provider or testing site to take a COVID-19 exam.

    Even if you don't begin to experience symptoms, its important still to self isolate and take a test to prevent further mitigation of the virus in the population.
